What Are We Learning Today?

  • Edit text and add pictures to a Google Slides presentation in three different ways.
  • Use Google Meet to share your screen and share links in the chatbox.
  • Use Think, Try, Ask to solve common tech problems before asking for help.
Success Criteria

How will you know you've succeeded?

★ Getting Started

I can open the Slides template from Classroom, add my name on Slide 1, and insert one image from the web.

★★ Levelling Up

I can insert images three different ways (web, device, camera) and share my screen on Google Meet.

★★★ Going Further

I can solve a tech problem on my own using Think, Try, Ask before raising my hand for help.

I Do

Editing Google Slides

Learn to create and describe content in Google Slides.

📂 Open from Classroom

Click the Google Slides template attached to the assignment.

✏️ Edit text

Click a text box, delete the placeholder text, and type your own.

🎨 Change font & colour

Highlight text, then change size and colour from the toolbar.

💡 Teacher says: "You are going to create your own content on these slides today."
I Do

Adding Pictures: 3 Methods

🌐 From the Web

Insert > Image > Search the web
Only use school-appropriate images!

💾 From Your Device

Insert > Image > Upload from computer
Find the file in your Downloads folder.

📷 From Your Camera

Insert > Image > Camera
Great for photographing your own work!

I Do

Sharing Your Screen on Google Meet

Use Google Meet features to communicate with your class.

1 Click 'Present now'

Find the button at the bottom of Google Meet.

2 Choose what to share

Select 'A tab' or 'Your entire screen'.

3 Show your work

Your classmates can now see your screen!

4 Stop sharing

Click 'Stop sharing' when you're done.

I Do

Using the Chatbox & Links

💬 The Chatbox

Open the chat panel in Google Meet. Type messages and share links with the class.

Rules: School messages only. No personal chats or silly comments!

🔗 Copy & Paste Links
Ctrl+C= Copy
Ctrl+V= Paste
🎯 Call & Response: Teacher says "Copy!" → Students say "Ctrl + C!" | Teacher says "Paste!" → Students say "Ctrl + V!"
I Do

Problem Solving: Think, Try, Ask

A strategy for when something goes wrong.

🧠 Think

What is the problem?
Read the error message. Look at what happened.

🔧 Try

Can I fix it myself?
Refresh the page. Check the internet. Try again.

✋ Ask

Tried twice and still stuck?
Raise my hand and ask for help.

🗣️ What would you do if your screen went blank during an online lesson? Discuss with your partner.
